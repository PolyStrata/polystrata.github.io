Let’s see how to create an audiogram step by step:

① Upload an audio file: On the Algo dashboard we support upload in multiple audio formats (like .wav, .mp3) & lengths from a few seconds (for an sound bite) to up to 30 minutes (for full episodes).

② Transform the audio into an editable transcript. The IBM Watson Speech to Text AI provides an accuracy of 98% for the English language & supports over 15 languages. This process returns an in-sync transcript of the audio that is editable through the Algo dashboard, so any typo can be easily fixed manually. We use the same AI to intelligently divide the audio into video scenes with length of 15-20 seconds, based on their semantic meaning.

③ Editing the modules. Users can edit the video timeline by simply editing the text (Descript style). Wrap the text & create a new line to create a new scene — or remove a line to merge two scenes into one.

Then, each scene can be turned into one of the template modules: ① Title ② Speaker (with waveform) ③ Media (video, image) ④ Maps ⑤ Charts. Bloomberg journalists use the dashboard to fill each module & create the podcast audiogram without any video skills required.
